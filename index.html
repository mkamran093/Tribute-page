Nichola Tesla


1856 - 1943

BioGraphy

Nikola Tesla (1856-1943) was an acclaimed inventor, electrical engineer, and physicist who played a vital role in the development of modern AC electrical power systems. Born in Croatia, Tesla received his education in engineering and physics in Europe before relocating to the United States. While initially collaborating with Thomas Edison, their differing views led to Tesla pursuing his own projects. Notably, he made groundbreaking contributions to AC power transmission and distribution by introducing the concept of the rotating magnetic field. Tesla held numerous patents, including the Tesla coil used in wireless power transfer and radio technology. He also explored wireless power transmission, wireless communication, and X-ray technology. Despite financial difficulties, Tesla's genius and forward-thinking continue to inspire scientists and engineers. His legacy as a brilliant inventor and pioneer in electrical power systems endures, shaping the modern world.
